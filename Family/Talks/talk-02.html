Part Two : The Eighties

1980s : a mixed decade

  • Mrs Thatcher, the Iron Lady, was in charge most of the time. So, still not a great time to be in England...
    • In 1982, we went to war with Argentina, over the Falklands. We won, for a change.
    • The Miners' strike of 1984 turned into a confrontation between government and unions; the government won.
    • In Ireland, the "Troubles" continued, with bombings, assassinations, and hunger strikes ...
    • The Thatcher government continued to shrink the state and privatise everything in sight
  • It wasn't all bad
    • The Rubik's Cube first appeared, as did Pac-Man, Super Mario, and Donkey Kong
    • IT spread out of the workplace into the home with a rash of "personal computers" : the IBM PC (1981); the Macintosh (1984); the Commodore 64 ; the BBC micro; the Amiga...
    • MTV was launched in the UK; also The Simpsons.
    • In 1981, Prince Charles and Lady Diana were married and everyone had a day off to watch
    • The World Wide Web was (allegedly) invented in 1989
    • The Berlin Wall fell and the Cold War started to thaw, kind of
  • We were property owners and I had a good job with proper pension rights!

Home Improvements

  • At this date, mortgage debt was also popular.
  • We lived in Plantation Road long enough, and my credit was good enough, for us to undertake various home improvements, like installing central heating (on George's recommendation), enlarging the kitchen, and adding a balcony at the back.
  • This is the back of the house at the start of the update process in June 1980
  • When works were completed, we gave a very good party for the builders, a local outfit led by Andy Wrigley.

French Holiday adventures

Pam is the only one still cheerful by the time we got to the Gare du Nord

  • In August 1980, we took Pam with us on what should have been a simple expedition to Cherbourg by boat and train.
  • Striking French fishermen complicated things by blockading the channel ports, so we travelled via Dover and Zeebrugge, Calais, and Paris instead, taking two days and nights, and making considerable use of my Flexible Friend.
  • (Remember that this was all in a distant age before the channel tunnel, before Eurostar, before cashpoints, before the euro)
  • But when we eventually got to our seaside bungalow near Barneville, the beaches, sunshine, sand dunes, French food were all just wonderful.

A veddink

Also wonderful, in its own way, was Grandma's wedding for which the girls got new pink dresses and lots of elderly relatives appeared. It was catered by a rather nice hotel in Windsor, and was my first encounter with beef wellington.

Financial wizardry

In 1982, we sold Plantation Road for £24,000. But moving on still required a new mortgage: I made it big enough to finance a holiday trip to Malawi as well. So we spent Christmas 1982 visiting friends and family in Limbe, Blantyre, and Zomba again.

Chez Alain, 10 Dec 1982

Onward to Walton Manor, March 1983

  • Fresh new mortgage in hand we moved decisively up the property ladder and into proper North Oxford, or Walton Manor at least.
  • Our new address, 110 Southmoor Rd, was a magnificent four storey victorian mansion, a snip at £53,000. It had been minimally improved by the vendors, but was in need of constant repair, which (in due course) we gave it.
  • We moved in on 25 March 1983, and stayed there (eventually) for the next 30 years.

Celebrations

  • Inter alia, there were school fetes and the Little Clarendon St Fair and the Jericho Festival..
  • In June 1986, there was a special street party to celebrate our street's hundredth birthday (or thereabouts).
  • I concocted and distributed a register of the inhabitants from old street directories. Belinda played in a scratch orchestra. There was tea and cake, and face painting, and bagpipes. Very North Oxford.
