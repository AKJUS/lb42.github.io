Part Four : The Noughties

The 2000s: Ho hum

  • The decade started well, in that the millenium bug never happened, whether because it was all a myth or because of the hard work of thousands of conscientious software engineers we'll never know
  • However, the headlines did not suggest this was a fun time for all
    • The 9/11 attacks on the World Trade Center were followed by the US invasion of Afghanistan, in which Tony Blair made us complicit
    • We were even more involved in the Gulf War that followed in 2003, ostensibly to remove "weapons of mass destruction" which were never found
    • Boxing Day 2004 was spoiled for millions by a veritable Tsunami in South East Asia
    • In July 2005, suicide bombers targetted London Transport, for the first time since the 1890s
    • An American bank called Lehman Brothers crashed in 2008: the world's financial systems were now so intertwined that this had disastrous effects on the UK economy too
    • And not just that Boris Johnson replaced Ken Livingstone as Lord Mayor Of London
  • On the slightly less gloomy side
    • In February 2004, Facebook was founded. It'll never catch on, we said.
    • In February 2005, Youtube was launched. What's the point of that, we said.

Tradition! Tradition! Tradition!

During the nineties and noughties, we had enough space to host the full family Christmas special, all the way from champagne breakfast beneath an over-decorated tree and endless unwrapping of presents, to massive lunch (usually involving a goose from Mr Feller, creamy Potatoes Tata-stylee, and flaming pud), le trou normand, and a little snooze before tea and cake.

It also usually involved additional boy friends and occasionally bemused hangers on. Up till 2005, there was room in the kitchen for everyone; in 2006, when Cousin Dave and Meili joined us, we moved it to the front basement.

Reason not the knees

While Christmas Shopping in 2007, I fell over in the High Street and broke something: a ligament in my left knee to be precise. Which in turn meant I spent New Years Eve in the JR labelled "nil by mouth" waiting to be operated on.

I spent most of January with one leg enclosed in a purple coffin, and the next six weeks learning how to bend my knee again.
